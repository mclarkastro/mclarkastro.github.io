About Me

Hi! My name is Max, or M, and my pronouns are they/them/theirs. I am a first year PhD student and Rackham Merit Fellow at Univeristy of Michigan - Ann Arbor in the Astronomy department. I recently completed my undergrad at University of Wisconsin - Madison with a B.S. in Astronomy-Physics. I am interested in studying exoplanets and the stars that they orbit. See my Research page to read more about what I've been working on!

Outside of research, I enjoy teaching and outreach. My work is better detailed in my C.V. page, but I am grateful to have had the opportunity to explore that aspect of my interests while an undergrad. As someone who grew up in a rural area and who holds various marginalized identities in higher ed, it is important to me that I play a role in increasing the accessibiltiy and equitabilty of education, especially astronomy, for those who hold similar and other marginalized identities.

While I am the type of person to make astronomy my entire personality, I do have passions outside of academia. The image below displays some of my interests. As a kid, I was always into creating. When I wasn't being challenged academically, I could be found doodling in the margins of every notebook I owned. While I don't draw as often anymore, I still engage in other forms of art, as shown in the upper left ("Kindness Rock" that I painted recently in my craft group) and bottom left (my favorite crochet project to date) below. I also love that I get to make my own body into artwork through tattoos and piercings.

When I'm not doing art or working, I can be found one of two places: hanging out with my friends' cats (and my friends, too, but isn't that cat so precious!), or going to concerts.
